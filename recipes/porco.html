Salmon with Beurre Blanc

Description

Porco Rosso is a film from Studio Ghibli featuring a pilot with a pig's head. That's all you need to know. This is a French sauce on salmon, a dish he eats in the movie. Three ingredients make something special.

Stupid simple to make. An easy dinner to impress guests that takes only a few ingredients. Keep it warm, don't reheat it, and eat within 24 hours.

Credits to Soph and Shirini of Uncultured Palate

Ingredients

  • 1 salmon seasoned as you please
  • 3 shallots
  • 2 1/4 (250g) sticks softened salted butter
  • 250 ml dry white wine

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F
  2. Fill pot quarter-full of water
  3. Boil on stove
  4. Chop shallots as small as possible without mincing
  5. Add white wine and shallots to pan on medium heat. Let reduce for 10 minutes
  6. Season salmon. Cover and let sit in oven for 15 minutes
  7. Slice pats of butter while waiting
  8. Transport reduction to bowl over pot. Add butter and whisk until blended. Keep warm
  9. Uncover salmon and let cook for another 15 minutes.
  10. Pour sauce on salmon.
